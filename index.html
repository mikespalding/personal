Four Days in
Music City

✈
🛣 Travel Day
Roll In, Get the Lay of the Land
No agenda, no museums, no rushing. Get to Nashville, find somewhere good to eat, and take a slow evening drive down Broadway to see the neon light up. Let the city sink in before the real sessions begin tomorrow.
🌆 Evening only🍗 Great night for hot chicken
Hattie B's for dinner →
01
🌅 Morning
Andrew Jackson's Hermitage
Start early — best before the June heat peaks. The 1,120-acre estate of the 7th President: restored mansion, formal gardens, family cemetery, and preserved slave quarters that tell the full, complicated story of his era.
📍 4580 Rachel's Lane⏰ Thur–Mon, opens 9am⌛ 2–3 hrs
thehermitage.com →
02
☀️ Afternoon
Fort Negley
The largest inland stone Civil War fort, built largely by enslaved and free Black Nashvillians. Short visit but punches well above its weight — striking skyline views and powerful history underfoot. Free to visit.
📍 1100 Fort Negley Blvd⏰ Tue–Sun⌛ 1 hr🆓 Free
Fort Negley Park →
🍖
🌇 Dinner
Peg Leg Porker
Cap the history day with Nashville's most celebrated BBQ — dry-rubbed ribs slow-smoked over hickory, pulled pork, sides that earn their own reputation. The Gulch is a short drive from Fort Negley. Get the ribs.
📍 903 Gleaves St, The Gulch⏰ Tue–Sun, Lunch & Dinner
peglegporker.com →
01
🌅 Morning
iHeart Studios on Music Row
Start the day on Music Row — where Bobby Bones broadcasts The Bobby Bones Show to 200+ stations every weekday 5–10am. No public tours, but standing outside the building where it happens is a real moment for any Bobby Bones fan.
📍 55 Music Square W⏰ Drive-by, morning⚠️ No public tours
bobbybones.iheart.com →
02
🌅 Morning
RCA Studio B
Right down the Row from iHeart — the most famous recording studio in country music history. Elvis, Dolly, the Everly Brothers, 35,000+ songs. One of the few Music Row studios open to the public. Book through the Country Music Hall of Fame.
📍 1611 Roy Acuff Pl⏰ Tours daily⌛ 1 hr🎟 Book via CMHOF
countrymusichalloffame.org →
03
☀️ Midday
Country Music Hall of Fame & Museum
The Smithsonian of country music — 350,000 sq ft of rhinestone costumes, vintage guitars, and two floors tracing the genre from folk roots to today. Studio B tickets are sold here, so stack them on the same visit.
📍 222 5th Ave S⏰ 9am–5pm daily⌛ 2–3 hrs
countrymusichalloffame.org →
04
🌆 Evening
Lower Broadway
End the day when Broadway is at its best — neon blazing, live music pouring out of every honky tonk. Walk slow. Dip into a bar, hear a song or two, move on. No cover. That's how locals do it.
📍 Broadway, Downtown⏰ Best after 7pm
visitmusiccity.com →
01
🌅 Morning
Ryman Auditorium
The Mother Church of Country Music. The guided backstage tour puts you in dressing rooms where Hank Williams and Dolly Parton prepared for the stage — and you get a free photo on it. Start here before the crowds build.
📍 116 5th Ave N⏰ Tours daily⌛ 1.5 hrs
ryman.com/tours →
02
☀️ Midday
Johnny Cash Museum
Walking distance from the Ryman. The world's most comprehensive collection of Cash artifacts — handwritten lyrics, personal items, awards, instruments, and the full arc from Dyess, Arkansas to "The Man in Black." Intense and unforgettable.
📍 119 3rd Ave S⏰ 9am–7pm⌛ 1.5 hrs
johnnycashmuseum.com →
🍗
🌞 Lunch
Nashville Hot Chicken
You cannot leave without it. Prince's invented it, Hattie B's perfected the experience. Order one level below your confidence. "Hot" here is not the same as "hot" anywhere else in the universe. Milk is your friend.
⚠️ Start at Medium🥛 Milk is your friend
Hattie B's →
03
🌇 Evening
Grand Ole Opry
The big finale. Chris Janson live or a backstage tour of the world's longest-running radio show — Saturday night at the Opry is exactly as good as it sounds. Book early.
📍 2804 Opryland Dr⏰ Saturday show⌛ 2–3 hrs
opry.com →
04
🎶 Bonus
The Bluebird Cafe
If the Opry is a tour rather than a show, swap the Bluebird in for the evening. Songwriter-in-the-round — intimate, no stage, just artists and their songs. Taylor Swift was discovered here. Seats are very limited — book well ahead.
📍 4104 Hillsboro Pike⏰ Shows nightly⌛ 1.5–2 hrs
bluebirdcafe.com →

Studio Notes

🎟

Book Ahead

Ryman, Opry, Hall of Fame, and Bluebird all sell out on June weekends. Buy tickets at least a week early — don't learn this the hard way.

👟

Wear Good Shoes

The Hall of Fame has three full floors. Fort Negley has uneven stone paths. Comfortable walking shoes are non-negotiable in June heat.

☀️

Beat the June Heat

Hot and humid. Do outdoor stops (Fort Negley, Hermitage) early morning. Hit museums through the hottest part of the day.

🍗

The Hot Chicken Rule

Order one spice level below what you think you can handle. "Hot" at Prince's is not the same as "Hot" anywhere else in the universe.

📷

The Shot List

Free Ryman stage photo (included in tour), Hall of Fame rotunda, Fort Negley skyline view. Hit all three. Post them to the Memory Wall.

🎸

Free Live Music

Lower Broadway bars have live bands all day — no cover. Walk in, hear a song or two, walk out. That's how locals do it.
